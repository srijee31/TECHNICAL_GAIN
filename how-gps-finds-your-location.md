# 🛰️ How GPS Finds Your Location

## 1. Introduction

GPS (Global Positioning System) allows your phone to determine its exact location anywhere on Earth.

It works using a network of **satellites orbiting the Earth**.

---

## 2. How GPS Works

There are around **30+ GPS satellites** constantly orbiting the Earth.

Your phone receives signals from multiple satellites and calculates its position.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Satellites Send Signals

Each satellite continuously sends signals containing:

- Satellite position
- Current time

---

### Step 2: Phone Receives Signals

Your phone receives signals from **at least 4 satellites**.

---

### Step 3: Distance Calculation

Your phone calculates the distance to each satellite based on how long the signal took to reach it.

---

### Step 4: Location Calculation

Using distances from multiple satellites, the phone calculates its **exact position on Earth**.

---

## 4. Real-Life Example

When you open **Google Maps**:

1. Your phone receives signals from satellites
2. It calculates your position
3. The location is displayed on the map

---

## 5. Simple Summary

GPS works by receiving signals from multiple satellites and calculating your location using the distance between your phone and those satellites.
