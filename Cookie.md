🍪 Technical Concept: What is a Cookie?

The reason the web doesn't have "amnesia."
🤔 Why do Cookies Exist?
Websites are naturally forgetful. Every time you click a link, the server "forgets" who you are. Without cookies:

You would have to log in on every single page.

Your shopping cart would reset every time you clicked "Next."

Your "Dark Mode" setting would turn off instantly.

Cookies exist to help websites remember small, specific details about you.

🍪 What Exactly is a Cookie?
In simple words:
A cookie is a tiny text file that a website asks your browser to save. It’s not a program; it's just a small piece of data.

🧠 The "Mall Stamp" Analogy 🖐️
The Entrance: You walk into a large mall or an amusement park.

The Stamp: Security stamps your hand so they know you’ve already paid.

The Return: You can walk in and out of different shops without explaining who you are every time.

The Stamp = The Cookie. It’s a small mark that proves you were already verified at the front gate.

🔄 How Cookies Work (The Loop)
The Visit: You go to a website (like Amazon).

The Gift: The Website sends a small cookie to your Browser.

The Storage: Your Browser saves that cookie on your phone or laptop.

The Handshake: Next time you visit, your Browser automatically sends that cookie back to the website.

The Recognition: The Website says, "Oh, it's Srijit! Welcome back."

📦 What do Cookies Actually Store?
Cookies are built to be very small and light. They usually hold:

Session IDs: A random string of letters/numbers that says "User is logged in."

Preferences: Like "Dark Mode: ON" or "Language: Kannada."

Tracking Info: Data about which products you looked at (this is why ads follow you around).

🔐 Are Cookies Dangerous?
By themselves, cookies are just text. They aren't viruses. However, they can be used for:

Tracking: Following your behavior across different websites (Third-party cookies).

Theft: If you are on an HTTP site (not secure), a hacker can "steal" your cookie and pretend to be you.

This is why HTTPS is mandatory for any site using cookies for login!

🧪 Phone-Friendly Experiment
Open your browser and log into a site (like Instagram or GitHub).

Close the browser tab completely.

Reopen the browser and go to the same site.

Still logged in? That is your cookie at work, telling the server who you are.

💡 One-Line Lock-in:
Cookies help websites remember you; without them, the web has amnesia.
