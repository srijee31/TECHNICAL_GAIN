# 🔳 How QR Codes Store Information

## 1. Introduction

A **QR Code (Quick Response Code)** is a two-dimensional barcode that stores information in a pattern of black and white squares.

It can store different types of data such as:

- Website URLs
- Text
- Payment information
- Contact details
- WiFi credentials

QR codes can be scanned quickly using a **smartphone camera or QR scanner**.

---

## 2. What Does QR Stand For?

QR stands for **Quick Response**.

The name comes from the ability of scanners to **read the code very quickly** compared to traditional barcodes.

QR codes were first developed in **1994 by Denso Wave (a Japanese company)** for tracking automotive parts.

---

## 3. Structure of a QR Code

A QR code contains several special patterns that help scanners understand the data.

Main parts of a QR code include:

| Component | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Finder Patterns | Help scanners detect the QR code position |
| Alignment Patterns | Ensure correct orientation |
| Timing Patterns | Help determine data grid size |
| Data Area | Stores the encoded information |

These patterns allow QR codes to be scanned **even if they are slightly rotated or partially damaged**.

---

## 4. How QR Codes Store Data

QR codes store information using **binary data (0s and 1s)**.

This binary data is represented by the **black and white squares** inside the code.

Example concept:


Black Square = 1
White Square = 0


The scanner reads this pattern and converts it back into meaningful information.

---

## 5. Step-by-Step Process When Scanning a QR Code

### Step 1: Camera Detects the QR Code

The phone camera identifies the **square pattern** of the QR code.

---

### Step 2: Scanner Reads the Pattern

The scanner analyzes the black and white modules (small squares).

---

### Step 3: Decode the Data

The scanner converts the pattern into **binary data** and then into readable information.

---

### Step 4: Perform the Action

Depending on the stored data, the phone may:

- Open a website
- Show text
- Connect to WiFi
- Open a payment app

Example:


QR Code → Scan → Decode → Open Website


---

## 6. Real-Life Examples

### UPI Payments

When you scan a payment QR code:

1. Your phone scans the code
2. The payment app reads the **merchant ID and payment details**
3. The app prepares a payment request
4. You confirm the payment

---

### WiFi Sharing

Some QR codes store WiFi credentials.

When scanned, the phone automatically connects to the network.

Example stored data:


WiFi Name: MyNetwork
Password: mypassword123


---

## 7. Advantages of QR Codes

- Fast scanning
- Stores more data than traditional barcodes
- Works even if partially damaged
- Easy to generate and use

---

## 8. Simple Summary

A **QR code stores information using patterns of black and white squares that represent binary data**.  
When scanned, a device decodes this pattern and converts it into readable information such as a website link, payment details, or text.
